FIR (First Information Report) is a written document prepared by police when information about a cognizable offense is received.

Key points:
- FIR can be filed at any police station.
- Police must record FIR for cognizable offenses.
- FIR can be filed by the victim or any person aware of the incident.

After FIR registration, a copy should be provided to the informant.

This information is for public awareness only and not legal advice.
